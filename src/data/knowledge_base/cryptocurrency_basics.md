# Cryptocurrency Basics

Cryptocurrency is a digital form of money that uses cryptography for security and operates on decentralized networks. Unlike traditional currencies issued by governments, most cryptocurrencies are not controlled by any single authority.

## What Is Cryptocurrency?

A cryptocurrency is a digital asset designed to work as a medium of exchange. Key characteristics include:

- **Digital only:** Cryptocurrencies exist entirely online. There are no physical coins or bills.
- **Decentralized:** Most operate on peer-to-peer networks without a central bank or government.
- **Limited supply:** Many cryptocurrencies have a fixed maximum supply, which can influence their value over time.
- **Pseudonymous:** Transactions are recorded publicly but are linked to wallet addresses rather than personal identities.

## Blockchain Basics

Blockchain is the technology that powers most cryptocurrencies. Think of it as a shared digital ledger:

- Every transaction is grouped into a "block."
- Each block is linked to the previous one, forming a "chain."
- The chain is stored across thousands of computers worldwide, making it extremely difficult to alter past records.
- This decentralized structure provides transparency and security without needing a trusted middleman like a bank.

## Bitcoin and Ethereum

### Bitcoin (BTC)
- Created in 2009 by the pseudonymous Satoshi Nakamoto.
- The first and most well-known cryptocurrency.
- Maximum supply of 21 million coins.
- Often described as "digital gold" — primarily used as a store of value.

### Ethereum (ETH)
- Launched in 2015 by Vitalik Buterin and others.
- Supports "smart contracts" — self-executing programs that run on the blockchain.
- Powers a large ecosystem of decentralized applications (dApps), including decentralized finance (DeFi) and NFTs.
- Does not have a fixed supply cap like Bitcoin.

Together, Bitcoin and Ethereum make up the majority of the total cryptocurrency market value.

## Volatility and Risk

Cryptocurrency is one of the most volatile asset classes available:

- **Extreme price swings** are common. It is not unusual for prices to move 10-20% in a single day.
- **Regulatory uncertainty:** Governments worldwide are still developing rules for crypto, which can cause sudden market shifts.
- **Security risks:** While blockchain itself is secure, exchanges and wallets can be hacked. Lost private keys mean permanently lost funds.
- **No fundamental backing:** Unlike stocks, most cryptocurrencies do not generate earnings or cash flow, making valuation highly speculative.
- **Scams and fraud:** The crypto space has a high prevalence of fraudulent projects, pump-and-dump schemes, and misleading promotions.

## Only Invest What You Can Afford to Lose

This is the most important rule for cryptocurrency investing:

- Treat crypto as a high-risk, speculative portion of your portfolio — not a core holding.
- Many financial educators suggest limiting crypto to no more than 5-10% of your total investment portfolio.
- Never invest emergency fund money, rent money, or funds you need in the short term.
- Be skeptical of anyone promising guaranteed returns in crypto.

## Key Takeaway

Cryptocurrency is an innovative but highly volatile and speculative asset class. Understanding the basics of blockchain, Bitcoin, and Ethereum is valuable financial literacy, but approach investing in crypto with caution, thorough research, and a clear understanding of the risks involved.
