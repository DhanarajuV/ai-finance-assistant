# Diversification: Don't Put All Your Eggs in One Basket

Diversification means spreading your investments across different assets to reduce risk.

## Why Diversify?

If you invest all your money in one stock and that company fails, you lose everything. If you spread across 100 stocks, one failure has minimal impact.

## Types of Diversification

1. **Across asset classes**: Mix stocks, bonds, and cash
2. **Across sectors**: Invest in technology, healthcare, finance, energy, etc.
3. **Across geographies**: Include domestic and international investments
4. **Across time**: Invest regularly (dollar-cost averaging) rather than all at once

## A Simple Diversified Portfolio

A common beginner allocation based on risk tolerance:

- **Conservative** (low risk): 30% stocks, 60% bonds, 10% cash
- **Moderate** (medium risk): 60% stocks, 30% bonds, 10% cash
- **Aggressive** (high risk): 80% stocks, 15% bonds, 5% cash

## The Role of Age

A common rule of thumb: subtract your age from 110 to get your stock percentage. A 30-year-old might hold 80% stocks and 20% bonds. A 60-year-old might hold 50% stocks and 50% bonds.

## Rebalancing

Over time, some investments grow faster than others, shifting your allocation. Rebalancing means periodically adjusting back to your target allocation, typically once or twice per year.
