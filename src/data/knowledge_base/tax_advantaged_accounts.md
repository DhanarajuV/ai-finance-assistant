# Tax-Advantaged Accounts

Tax-advantaged accounts help you reduce taxes on your investments.

## Tax-Deferred Accounts

Contributions may reduce your taxable income today. You pay taxes when you withdraw.

- **Traditional 401(k)**: Employer-sponsored. 2024 limit: $23,000 ($30,500 if 50+).
- **Traditional IRA**: Individual account. 2024 limit: $7,000 ($8,000 if 50+).
- **403(b)**: For nonprofit and government employees. Similar to 401(k).

## Tax-Free Growth Accounts

Contributions are made with after-tax money. Withdrawals in retirement are tax-free.

- **Roth 401(k)**: Employer-sponsored Roth option.
- **Roth IRA**: Individual account. Income limits apply for eligibility.

## Other Tax-Advantaged Accounts

- **HSA (Health Savings Account)**: Triple tax advantage — tax-deductible contributions, tax-free growth, tax-free withdrawals for medical expenses. Often called the best tax-advantaged account available.
- **529 Plan**: Tax-free growth for education expenses.

## Choosing the Right Account

1. Get your full employer 401(k) match first (free money)
2. Max out HSA if eligible (triple tax benefit)
3. Contribute to Roth IRA if income-eligible
4. Go back and max out 401(k)
5. Use taxable brokerage account for additional investing
