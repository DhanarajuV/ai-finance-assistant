# What Are ETFs (Exchange-Traded Funds)?

An ETF is a basket of investments (stocks, bonds, or other assets) that trades on a stock exchange like a single stock.

## How ETFs Work

Instead of buying individual stocks, you buy one ETF that holds many stocks. For example, an S&P 500 ETF holds shares of all 500 companies in the S&P 500 index.

## Why ETFs Are Popular

1. **Diversification**: One purchase gives you exposure to many companies, reducing risk.
2. **Low cost**: ETFs typically have lower fees than mutual funds.
3. **Easy to trade**: You can buy and sell ETFs throughout the day, just like stocks.
4. **Transparency**: ETF holdings are published daily.

## Common Types

- **Index ETFs**: Track a market index like the S&P 500 (e.g., VOO, SPY).
- **Bond ETFs**: Hold a collection of bonds (e.g., BND, AGG).
- **Sector ETFs**: Focus on specific industries like technology or healthcare.

## ETFs vs Mutual Funds

Both hold baskets of investments, but ETFs trade like stocks during market hours while mutual funds only trade once per day at market close. ETFs usually have lower expense ratios.

## Getting Started

Many financial advisors recommend beginners start with a broad market index ETF because it provides instant diversification at low cost.
