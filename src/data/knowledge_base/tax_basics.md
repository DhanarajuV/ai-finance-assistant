# Tax Basics for Investors

Understanding taxes on investments is essential for maximizing your returns.

## Types of Investment Taxes

### Capital Gains Tax
When you sell an investment for more than you paid, the profit is a capital gain.

- **Short-term capital gains**: Assets held less than 1 year. Taxed as ordinary income (10%-37% depending on your tax bracket).
- **Long-term capital gains**: Assets held more than 1 year. Taxed at preferential rates: 0%, 15%, or 20% depending on income.

### Dividend Tax
- **Qualified dividends**: Taxed at long-term capital gains rates (0%, 15%, or 20%).
- **Ordinary dividends**: Taxed as regular income.

### Interest Income
Interest from bonds and savings accounts is taxed as ordinary income.

## Tax-Loss Harvesting

If you sell an investment at a loss, you can use that loss to offset capital gains. If losses exceed gains, you can deduct up to $3,000 per year from ordinary income. Remaining losses carry forward to future years.

## The Wash Sale Rule

You cannot claim a tax loss if you buy the same or a substantially identical security within 30 days before or after the sale.
