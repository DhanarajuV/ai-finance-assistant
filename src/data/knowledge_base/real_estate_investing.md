# Introduction to Real Estate Investing

Real estate is one of the oldest and most popular asset classes for building wealth. Today, you do not need to buy a physical property to invest in real estate — several accessible options exist for investors at every level.

## REITs (Real Estate Investment Trusts)

A REIT is a company that owns, operates, or finances income-producing real estate. REITs trade on stock exchanges just like regular stocks, making them easy to buy and sell.

- **How they work:** REITs are required by law to distribute at least 90% of their taxable income to shareholders as dividends, which often results in attractive yields.
- **Types of REITs:** Residential, commercial, healthcare, industrial, data centers, and more.
- **Minimum investment:** As low as the price of a single share (or less with fractional shares).

## Real Estate Crowdfunding

Crowdfunding platforms pool money from many investors to fund real estate projects.

- **Platforms** like Fundrise, RealtyMogul, and CrowdStreet offer access to deals that were once available only to wealthy investors.
- **Minimum investments** typically range from $500 to $25,000 depending on the platform.
- **Returns** come from rental income and property appreciation, but your money is usually locked up for several years.

## Rental Properties vs. REITs

| Factor | Rental Property | REITs |
|---|---|---|
| Control | Full control over the property | No control — professional management |
| Liquidity | Very low — selling takes weeks or months | High — sell shares instantly on an exchange |
| Capital required | Large (down payment, closing costs, repairs) | Small (price of one share) |
| Effort | Active — tenant management, maintenance | Passive — no hands-on work |
| Tax benefits | Depreciation, mortgage interest deductions | Dividend income, often taxed as ordinary income |

## Diversification Benefits

Real estate often behaves differently from stocks and bonds, which makes it a valuable diversification tool:

- Real estate returns have a low correlation with stock market returns over many periods.
- Rental income and REIT dividends can provide steady cash flow regardless of stock market conditions.
- Real estate can serve as a partial hedge against inflation, since property values and rents tend to rise with prices over time.

## Liquidity Considerations

Liquidity — how quickly you can convert an investment to cash — varies widely in real estate:

- **REITs:** Highly liquid. Buy and sell during market hours like any stock.
- **Crowdfunding:** Illiquid. Your money is typically locked for 3 to 7 years.
- **Physical property:** Very illiquid. Selling involves agents, inspections, and closing processes that take weeks to months.

Always consider how soon you might need access to your money before choosing a real estate investment.

## Key Takeaway

Real estate investing offers income, diversification, and inflation protection. REITs make it accessible to beginners with small amounts of capital, while rental properties and crowdfunding offer different risk-reward profiles. Match your choice to your budget, time horizon, and willingness to be hands-on.
