# What Are Stocks?

A stock represents a share of ownership in a company. When you buy a stock, you become a partial owner of that company.

## How Stocks Work

Companies sell stocks to raise money for their business. In return, stockholders may benefit in two ways:

1. **Price appreciation**: If the company does well, the stock price may increase, and you can sell for a profit.
2. **Dividends**: Some companies share profits with stockholders through regular cash payments called dividends.

## Key Terms

- **Share**: A single unit of stock.
- **Ticker symbol**: A short code used to identify a stock (e.g., AAPL for Apple).
- **Market capitalization**: The total value of all a company's shares. Large-cap companies (over $10 billion) are generally considered more stable.

## Risks

Stock prices can go down. You could lose some or all of your investment. Stocks are generally more volatile than bonds but have historically provided higher long-term returns.

## Example

If you buy 10 shares of a company at $50 each, you invest $500. If the price rises to $60, your investment is worth $600 — a 20% gain. If it drops to $40, your investment is worth $400 — a 20% loss.
