# Retirement Accounts: 401(k) and IRA

Retirement accounts are special investment accounts that offer tax advantages to encourage saving for retirement.

## 401(k)

A 401(k) is an employer-sponsored retirement plan.

- **Contribution limit (2024)**: $23,000 per year ($30,500 if age 50+)
- **Employer match**: Many employers match a percentage of your contributions — this is free money
- **Tax benefit**: Contributions reduce your taxable income today. You pay taxes when you withdraw in retirement.
- **Penalty**: Withdrawing before age 59½ typically incurs a 10% penalty plus taxes.

## IRA (Individual Retirement Account)

An IRA is a retirement account you open yourself, independent of your employer.

### Traditional IRA
- Contributions may be tax-deductible
- You pay taxes on withdrawals in retirement
- Contribution limit (2024): $7,000 ($8,000 if age 50+)

### Roth IRA
- Contributions are made with after-tax money (no tax deduction today)
- Withdrawals in retirement are TAX-FREE
- Same contribution limits as Traditional IRA
- Income limits apply for eligibility

## Which Should You Choose?

1. **Always get your employer's 401(k) match first** — it's free money
2. **If you expect higher taxes in retirement**: Choose Roth IRA
3. **If you want tax savings now**: Choose Traditional IRA or 401(k)

## The Power of Starting Early

Investing $500/month starting at age 25 with 7% average returns grows to approximately $1.2 million by age 65. Starting at 35, the same investment grows to only about $567,000. Time is your greatest advantage.
