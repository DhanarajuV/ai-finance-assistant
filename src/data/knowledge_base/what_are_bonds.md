# What Are Bonds?

A bond is a loan you make to a company or government. In return, they promise to pay you back with interest over a set period.

## How Bonds Work

When you buy a bond, the issuer agrees to:
1. Pay you regular interest payments (called **coupon payments**)
2. Return your original investment (called **face value** or **par value**) when the bond matures

## Types of Bonds

- **Government bonds (Treasury bonds)**: Issued by the U.S. government. Considered very safe.
- **Municipal bonds**: Issued by state and local governments. Often tax-exempt.
- **Corporate bonds**: Issued by companies. Higher risk but higher interest rates.

## Key Terms

- **Yield**: The annual return you earn from a bond's interest payments.
- **Maturity**: The date when the bond issuer returns your principal.
- **Credit rating**: A grade (AAA to D) indicating how likely the issuer is to repay. Higher ratings mean lower risk.

## Bonds vs Stocks

Bonds are generally less risky than stocks but offer lower returns. They provide steady income and are often used to balance a portfolio.
